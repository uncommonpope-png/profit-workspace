Introducing the PLT Framework

PLT stands for Profit, Love, Tax - a simple scoring system that transforms how you make business decisions.

Here's how it works:

  • PROFIT = What you gain (money, leverage, position, assets)
  • LOVE = Relationship capital built (trust, alliances, community)
  • TAX = Real costs paid (time, energy, money, stress)

Formula: SOUL PROFIT = PROFIT + LOVE - TAX

How to Apply PLT Scoring

For any business decision, rate each factor on a scale of 1-10:

Step 1: Score PROFIT (1-10)

  • 1-3: Minimal gains, mostly learning experience
  • 4-6: Decent financial returns or strategic value
  • 7-9: Strong gains, high priority opportunity
  • 10: Life-changing, game-shifting opportunity

Step 2: Score LOVE (1-10)

  • 1-3: Purely transactional, no relationship building
  • 4-6: Some trust/rapport development
  • 7-9: Strong relationship potential, mutual value
  • 10: Game-changing partnership or alliance

Step 3: Score TAX (1-10)

  • 1-3: Minimal investment, easy commitment
  • 4-6: Moderate time, energy, or financial cost
  • 7-9: High investment required, significant sacrifice
  • 10: Extremely costly, major opportunity cost

Step 4: Calculate Your SOUL PROFIT

Add PROFIT + LOVE, then subtract TAX. Your decision threshold:

  • 10+: Excellent opportunity, prioritize immediately
  • 6-9: Good opportunity, likely worth pursuing
  • 2-5: Neutral, consider alternatives or negotiate
  • Below 2: Poor opportunity, likely decline

Advanced PLT Applications

Pricing Strategy

Use PLT to evaluate pricing decisions. Higher prices that reduce TAX (fewer difficult clients) while maintaining PROFIT and LOVE often score better than low-margin work.

Time Allocation

Score how you spend your hours. Most entrepreneurs discover they're doing too many low-score activities and not enough high-score ones.

Team Building

Evaluate hiring decisions, partnership invitations, and collaboration opportunities using PLT scoring.

Investment Decisions

Whether it's new equipment, software, or business investments, PLT helps quantify the true value beyond just financial returns.

Common PLT Mistakes to Avoid

1. Emotional Scoring

Don't inflate scores because you want something to work. Be brutally honest about costs and realistic about benefits.

2. Ignoring Opportunity Cost

When scoring TAX, include what you're giving up by saying yes to this opportunity.

3. Short-term Thinking

Consider long-term LOVE benefits. A lower-profit opportunity that builds strong relationships often pays off later.

4. Inconsistent Scoring

Develop personal benchmarks. What does a "7 PROFIT" look like for your business? Stay consistent.
