The PLT Automation Principle

The 5-Phase Business Automation Roadmap

1

Process Audit — Map Before You Build

Document every recurring task that takes more than 30 minutes per week. Use a simple spreadsheet: task name, frequency, time per occurrence, and PLT score. This audit typically reveals 8–12 high-priority automation targets.

2

Lead Generation Automation

Automate top-of-funnel activity first — it compounds the fastest. Set up automated outreach sequences, lead capture forms with immediate follow-up, and content distribution pipelines that run without manual input.

3

Sales Pipeline Automation

Automate CRM updates, follow-up reminders, proposal generation, and payment collection. Every step that currently requires a human decision should be evaluated — many can be handled by conditional logic and templates.

4

Customer Service Automation

Deploy AI chatbots for FAQ handling, automated onboarding sequences, and triggered support emails. Keep human touchpoints for high-Love moments — negotiations, complaints, and relationship-building conversations.

5

Operations & Reporting Automation

Automate your invoicing, payroll, inventory alerts, and weekly reporting. Connect your tools via APIs or no-code platforms so data flows automatically without manual entry or copy-paste workflows.

Essential Automation Tools for Entrepreneurs

No-Code Workflow Automation

Zapier, Make (formerly Integromat), and n8n connect your existing tools without writing code. Start with one trigger-action pair — for example, "New lead in form → Add to CRM → Send welcome email" — before building complex multi-step workflows.

AI-Powered Customer Interaction

Modern AI tools (including those using GPT-4 and Claude APIs) can handle initial customer inquiries, qualify leads, and provide personalized responses at scale. The key is to train your AI on your actual business language and PLT-scored responses.

Email Marketing Automation

Automate email sequences triggered by user behavior — sign-up, purchase, inactivity, or specific page visits. Behavior-triggered emails outperform broadcast emails by 3–8x because they are timely and relevant.

Social Media and Content Distribution

Use scheduling tools to maintain consistent publishing without daily manual effort. Repurpose content automatically across platforms. One piece of content — an article, a video script, a book excerpt — can feed 5–7 distribution channels with the right automation setup.

Common Automation Mistakes to Avoid

  • Automating a broken process. Automation amplifies what's already there. Fix the process before you automate it.
  • Over-automating relationship touchpoints. High-PLT-Love moments need human presence. Automating them destroys the relationship capital you've built.
  • Building automation before validating the offer. Don't build an automated sales funnel for a product that hasn't been validated manually first.
  • Ignoring maintenance Tax. Every automation system requires monitoring and updating. Factor this into your PLT score before building.
  • Connecting too many tools too fast. Each integration adds complexity and a potential failure point. Build incrementally and test each layer.

Measuring Automation ROI with PLT

Every automation you build should improve your PLT score over time. Track these metrics monthly:

  • Profit: Revenue per hour of manual effort, customer lifetime value, conversion rates at each funnel stage
  • Love: Customer satisfaction scores, response times, retention rates, referral rates
  • Tax: Tool costs, maintenance hours, error rates, technical debt

If your automation stack is improving Profit and Love while keeping Tax low, you are building correctly. If Tax is rising without corresponding Profit gains, you have over-engineered your stack.
