Why Business Credit Matters in 2026

Business credit is your company's financial reputation. Unlike personal credit, business credit has no legal liability limits—meaning you can access significantly more capital without personal guarantees.

PLT Reality: Strong business credit = $100K+ in available capital without personal liability. That's massive PROFIT potential with reduced personal TAX.

The PLT Approach to Business Credit

Most entrepreneurs mix personal and business credit, creating massive TAX liability. The PLT framework demands separation and strategic building:

💰 PROFIT Analysis

  • Access to business loans without personal guarantee
  • Higher credit limits than personal cards
  • Better terms and lower interest rates
  • Equipment financing opportunities
  • Business credit cards with rewards

❤️ LOVE Factor

  • Protects personal relationships from business debt
  • Builds trust with suppliers and partners
  • Creates professional credibility
  • Reduces stress on family finances

📉 TAX Considerations

  • Time to establish (6-12 months)
  • Initial fees and deposits
  • Ongoing account maintenance
  • Personal guarantees during early stages

The Betty Credits 6-Step Business Credit System

1Legal Business Foundation

Your business needs legal recognition before credit bureaus will track it.

  • Form LLC or Corporation (not sole proprietorship)
  • Get Federal EIN (free from IRS.gov)
  • Register with your state
  • Get any required business licenses
PLT Tip: LLC offers liability protection (reduces TAX) while being simpler than corporations. Best PROFIT/TAX ratio for most businesses.

2Get Your DUNS Number

D&B (Dun & Bradstreet) tracks business credit. Your DUNS number is your business credit social security number.

  • Apply free at dnb.com
  • Use business address, not home
  • Make sure info is consistent everywhere
  • Consider expedited service if you need it fast

3Establish Business Banking

Separate business banking is required for business credit tracking.

  • Open business checking account
  • Use EIN, not SSN
  • Maintain consistent business name
  • Keep business and personal completely separate
Betty's Rule: Never mix business and personal banking. The IRS sees this as proof of personal liability—massive TAX increase.

4Get a Business Phone and Address

Credit bureaus verify businesses through public listings.

  • Business phone line (can forward to cell)
  • Business address (virtual office works)
  • List in 411, Google My Business
  • Get business website (even simple one-pager)

6Graduate to Business Credit Cards

Once you have trade lines established, apply for business credit cards.

  • Start with cards that don't require personal guarantee
  • Use business EIN, not SSN when possible
  • Keep utilization under 30%
  • Pay in full each month
Best First Cards: Chase Ink Business, Capital One Spark, American Express Business cards often approve with 6+ months business history.

Advanced Business Credit Strategies

The Credit Stacking Method

Once established, you can "stack" multiple credit lines:

  1. Business credit cards (revolving credit)
  2. Equipment financing (installment credit)
  3. Business lines of credit
  4. SBA loans (with strong credit history)

Using PLT to Evaluate Credit Offers

Every business credit offer should pass the PLT test:

  • PROFIT: Will this credit line generate more than it costs?
  • LOVE: Does this strengthen business relationships or create stress?
  • TAX: What's the true cost including time, fees, and opportunity cost?
SOUL_PROFIT = PROFIT + LOVE - TAX
Only take business credit with positive SOUL_PROFIT score above 7.

Common Business Credit Mistakes (High TAX)

  • Mixing personal and business credit
  • Using SSN instead of EIN on applications
  • Inconsistent business name across accounts
  • Not paying net-30 vendors on time
  • Applying for too much credit too fast
  • Personal guaranteeing everything

Timeline and Expectations

Building business credit takes time. Here's the typical PLT-optimized timeline:

  • Month 1: Form business, get EIN, DUNS, banking
  • Month 2-3: Establish 3-4 net-30 vendor accounts
  • Month 4-6: Pay vendors on time, build payment history
  • Month 7-9: Apply for first business credit cards
  • Month 10-12: Scale credit lines and apply for larger financing
