How to Choose: The PLT Decision Framework

Apply the PLT test before committing to any automation platform:

  1. Profit test: Will this tool automate a task that directly generates revenue or saves measurable time? If yes, how many hours per week — and what is that time worth to you?
  2. Love test: Will you actually use this tool long-term? Does it integrate with tools you already like? Will the automations it creates improve your customer experience?
  3. Tax test: What is the monthly cost, the learning curve, and the maintenance burden? Can you maintain these automations yourself, or will you be dependent on a developer?

The tool with the highest PLT score for your specific situation is the right tool — regardless of what's trending or what competitors use.

AI Workflow Automation in 2026: What's Changed

The biggest shift in 2026 is that AI is now a first-class citizen in workflow automation — not just an add-on. Modern workflows can include AI decision nodes that analyze incoming data and choose different paths, draft responses, classify content, or generate personalized outputs at scale.

This changes the calculus significantly. A workflow that would have required complex conditional logic two years ago can now be handled by a single AI node with a well-crafted prompt. The Tax of building sophisticated automations has dropped dramatically.

Getting Your First Automation Live Today

The best automation is the one that actually runs. Here's how to get your first workflow live in under an hour:

  1. Pick your highest-Tax manual task — the one that takes the most time per week
  2. Create a free Zapier account and search for your two apps (e.g., Gmail + Google Sheets)
  3. Set up the simplest possible trigger-action pair
  4. Test with a real example and verify the output is correct
  5. Turn it on and run it for one week before building the next automation
