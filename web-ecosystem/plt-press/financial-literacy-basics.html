Betty's Beginner Money Rules

Simple rules that prevent 90% of financial mistakes:

Rule 1: Pay Yourself First

Before any other expense, save at least 10% of your income. This is PROFIT you're building, not TAX you're paying.

Rule 2: The PLT Credit Rule

Only borrow money if the PROFIT is clear and measurable. Credit cards for convenience, never for living expenses you can't afford.

Rule 3: Emergency Fund = Peace of Mind

3-6 months of expenses in savings. This is LOVE insurance—it protects your relationships from financial stress.

Rule 4: Invest in What You Understand

Start simple: index funds, 401(k), basic investments. Complex investments = high TAX risk.

Rule 5: Track Everything for 30 Days

You can't manage what you don't measure. Track every dollar to see where your money really goes.

Common Beginner Financial Mistakes

Mistake 1: No Emergency Fund

Living paycheck to paycheck creates massive TAX—stress, bad decisions, and expensive emergencies.

Mistake 2: Credit Card Debt

20%+ interest rates destroy PROFIT. Pay credit cards in full every month, always.

Mistake 3: Not Investing Early

Compound interest is the most powerful force in finance. Starting late costs hundreds of thousands in lost PROFIT.

Mistake 4: Lifestyle Inflation

Spending more as you earn more prevents wealth building. Keep lifestyle increases below income increases.

Mistake 5: No Financial Education

Financial illiteracy is expensive. Invest in learning—it pays the highest return.
